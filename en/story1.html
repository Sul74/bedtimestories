🌙 A Gentle Bedtime Story

Once upon a quiet night,

When stars twinkled softly and the wind whispered lullabies, there was a little Moon, round and bright, hanging in the sky all alone.

The Moon watched over the sleeping world below— the sleepy bears in their caves, the cozy kittens curled in baskets, and even the tiny children dreaming in their beds.

But tonight, the Moon felt a little lonely. He didn’t want to shine. He didn’t want to glow. He just wanted… a hug.

So the Moon floated a little lower.

First, he peeked into a forest, where the owls hooted gently.

"Hello?" said the Moon. "Could someone give me a hug?"

The owls blinked slowly and said, “Oh sweet Moon, we only flap and fly. But we’ll hoot a happy tune for you.”

The Moon smiled a little, but he still wished for a hug.

Next, he drifted over the ocean, where dolphins leaped in silver sparkles.

"Could someone here hug me?" asked the Moon.

The dolphins chirped and twirled. “We cannot hug you, Moon,” they sang, “but we’ll splash joyfully for you!”

The Moon felt warmer, but it wasn’t quite the same as a hug.

Finally, the Moon floated gently to a quiet village, where a little child was just falling asleep.

The child looked out the window and saw the Moon, glowing soft and sad.

Without a word, the child opened their arms wide, hugging the night sky with their little heart.

The Moon didn’t need to be touched to feel it. The warmth, the love, the quiet kindness— it wrapped around him like a blanket.

The Moon beamed softly, happier than ever before. He rose back into the sky, full and bright, hugged by love and ready to light up dreams.

And so,

the child slept with a smile, and the Moon watched over them — no longer lonely, just loved.

Goodnight, little star. 🌟 The Moon is smiling just for you.
